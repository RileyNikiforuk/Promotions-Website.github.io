How to setup your Xbox

Physical setup

Step 1: Unbox the console

Components included with the original Xbox One

An Xbox One game system typically includes:

1. Xbox One console

2. Kinect sensor (optional)

3. Power supply

4. Mains lead

5. Xbox One Wireless Controller

6. Two AA batteries

7. One HDMI cable

8. Documents (Quick Start Guide, manual, Xbox subscription trial card)

Components included with the Xbox One X, Xbox One S, and Xbox One S all-digital edition

An Xbox One X, Xbox One S, and Xbox One S All-Digital Edition game bundle typically includes:

1. Xbox One console

2. Mains lead

3. Xbox One Wireless Controller

4. Two AA batteries

5. One HDMI cable

6. Optional vertical stand (included with some models, and also available for purchase separately)

7. Documentation

Note Some game bundles may come with other items like games or Xbox trial subscription offers.

Step 2: Position the console

Original Xbox One console

Note: If you have an original Xbox One console, before you do anything else, remove the protective tape over the disc tray opening on the front left side of the console.

An original Xbox One console should only be placed horizontally, never vertically. Make sure that you place your console on a stable surface that is well-ventilated, relatively cool, and away from direct heat sources. Don't put other objects on, under, or right next to the console. An object on the top right of the console might block its heat vents.

Make sure that you place your console on a stable surface that is well-ventilated, relatively cool, and away from direct heat sources. Don't put other objects on, under, or right next to the console. An object on the top right of the console might block its heat vents.

Step 3: Connect your console to a TV or a monitor

The Xbox One console system includes one HDMI cable. You can use this cable to connect the console to an HDMI-compatible TV, HDTV, or monitor. Here's how:

1. Find the HDMI ports on the back of the console. They are flat, rectangular openings near the middle of the console with another square port between them.

2. Connect the HDMI cable to the HDMI OUT port on your console (the leftmost HDMI port on the back of your console). Do not connect the HDMI cable to the HDMI IN port on your console.

HDMI ports on Original Xbox One console                                    HDMI ports on Xbox One X, Xbox One S, and Xbox One S All-Digital                                                                                                           Edition consoles

3. Connect the other end of the HDMI cable to the HDMI input on your TV. Note that video output from the Xbox One console is HDMI only.

4. If you’re also connecting your console to a sound system, see: Connect your Xbox console to a sound system

Note: The following section only applies to Xbox One X, Xbox One S, or Xbox One S All-Digital Edition consoles. If you have an original Xbox One console, or if your TV does not support 4K or HDR10 formats, skip to Step 4 below.

If you’re connecting your console to a sound system, you’ll also need to ensure that the sound system has an HDMI 2.0 input and is rated for 4K passthrough.

If the HDMI cable included with your Xbox isn’t long enough, we recommend that you find an “HDMI Premium Certified Cable” of the appropriate length. If you need a cable that’s 50 feet or longer, you may want an “Active High-Speed HDMI Cable.”

Make sure that all the cables on the back of your console are gathered away from and are not touching the console. If you must run the cables to the side, avoid running cables by the back-left side of the console by placing the console vertically on the stand. This helps avoid wireless interference that can affect the console’s ability to reliably connect to Wi-Fi or decrease your wireless controller’s range.

It’s common that TVs support 4K at 60 Hz on only one HDMI input. Check your TV’s instruction manual or manufacturer’s website to verify which HDMI input to use.

To display all content in 4K, your TV must support 4K at 60 Hz resolution. To display HDR (High Dynamic Range) content, your TV must support the HDR10 format. It’s common for one or both of these features to be disabled in your TV’s settings. Check your TV’s instruction manual or manufacturer’s website to verify which settings need to be enabled to display 4K and HDR content.

If you're having problems with your 4K or HDR display, see: Troubleshooting 4k and HDR

Step 4: Connect your set-top box to the Xbox console (optional)

If you don't have cable or satellite TV, skip this step.

If you connect the set-top box (STB) for your cable or satellite TV provider to your console, you can control your live TV content through your Xbox One. Here's how:

1. On your TV, disconnect the HDMI cable that connects your TV to your STB.

1. Connect the cable to the HDMI IN port on your console (the rightmost of the HDMI ports on the back of your console).

Note: The HDMI input on the Xbox console is an HDMI 1.4 port and can’t carry a 4K signal. This means that if you have a 4K set-top box (STB) for your cable or satellite TV, you should skip this step and instead plug your STB directly into your TV if you want to get the most out of live TV.

Step 5: Connect your console to your router or modem (wired connection only)

You can connect your Xbox One console to your wireless router or modem to access the internet through your console. If you want to connect wirelessly, skip this step. You’ll be prompted to connect to a wireless network later, during digital setup. For a wired connection, use an Ethernet cable to connect your router or modem to the networking port on the right side of the back of the console.

Step 6: Connect the console to a kinect sensor (optional)

Original Xbox One console

Note: The cable for the Kinect sensor is 3 metres (9 feet) long. This is the longest cable available, so make sure your Xbox One and your Kinect sensor are close enough to connect them.

Xbox One X, Xbox One S, and Xbox One S all-digital edition consoles

To use an Xbox One Kinect sensor with an Xbox One X, Xbox One S, or Xbox One S All-Digital Edition console, you'll need an Xbox Kinect Adaptor, which lets you connect your Xbox One Kinect sensor to your console.

Note: The Xbox 360 Kinect sensor is not compatible with any Xbox One console.

To learn how to set up and use an Xbox One Kinect sensor with your Xbox One console, see: Setup tips for your kinect sensor play space

Step 7: Connect a USB 3.0 external storage device (optional)

requirements

You can use an external hard drive to store games, apps, and console settings for your new Xbox One.

The external hard drive must:

Use a USB 3.0 connection. If you’re using a USB hub, the hub must also be USB 3.0 or later.

Have at least 128 GB of storage.

Have a partition. Typically, drives come with a preformatted partition. For more information on partitioning an external hard drive, refer to the manufacturer’s instructions.

External hard drives that don’t meet these requirements are treated as storage for media like music or video. For more information, see: Manage storage on your Xbox console

New users to Xbox One

Once you complete the digital setup of your new console, your Xbox prompts you to format your external hard drive if you want to use it to store your Xbox games and apps. If you want to use this drive for personal media such as pictures, music, and video, you can choose to keep the drive as media storage and the drive will not be formatted.

Important: Make sure you don’t have any important information stored on your external hard drive before you select Format for games & apps in Step 3 below. Reformatting an external hard drive makes all data already stored on it unrecoverable.

If you’re not prompted to format the drive, or you choose to cancel formatting, you can set it up later:

1. Press the Xbox button to open the guide.

2. Select Profile & system > Settings > System > Storage devices.

3. Select your external drive, and then select Format for games & apps.

If your Xbox doesn’t recognise your external drive, see: Troubleshoot external storage detection on Xbox

Note: Your Xbox console can only recognise three external hard drives at a time.

User upgrading or adding an additional Xbox One

If you’re upgrading or adding an additional Xbox One, using an external hard drive can make digital setup faster and simpler. You can back up your settings and copy all your favorite games and apps from your original Xbox One to this external hard drive. For instructions on how to back up your settings, see: Back up your settings on Xbox

Important: Backups don’t include settings for things that might be different on your new Xbox, such as console name or the ability to control a specific TV, cable box, or audio-video receiver.

Physical connections

There are three USB 3.0 ports on the Xbox One console: two on the back, and one either on the front (Xbox One S, Xbox One X, and All-Digital Edition) or on the left side (original Xbox One). Connect the external hard drive to any of the available USB 3.0 ports. For more information on using additional USB devices with your Xbox One, see: Using the USB ports on your Xbox console

Step 8: Plug in the power supply or mains lead (optional)

1. Follow these steps, depending on what kind of console you have:

On an Original Xbox One console, plug the power supply into the leftmost port on the back of the console, and then plug the mains lead into the power supply.

On an Xbox One X, Xbox One S, or Xbox One S All-Digital Edition console, plug the mains lead into the leftmost port on the back of the console.

2. Plug the other end of the mains lead into an electrical outlet.

3. Make sure all the cables on the back of your console are gathered away from the console so they’re not touching it.

Digital setup

Step 1: Power on your console for the first time

Make sure your Xbox controller is handy and that there are fresh batteries in it. If you turned on your console by using the Xbox button  on the front of the console, you might see an image of the Xbox controller with the Xbox button flashing to remind you to press the Xbox button in the centre of the controller to turn it on.

To turn on your Xbox console and begin the digital setup experience, press either of these buttons:

The Xbox button on the front-right side of the console

The Xbox button in the centre of the controller

Things to keep in mind:

If your controller isn’t already paired with your console, you can connect your controller by pressing and holding the Pair button ((( on each. For more info, see: Connect a wireless controller to your Xbox

Your controller is how you navigate around your Xbox console. Remember to use the directional pad + to highlight any setting that you want to select, and then press the A button to select that setting and save your choice. To find out how to use the Xbox Wireless Controller, visit: Get to know your Xbox controller

Press the A button to continue with your digital setup.

Step 2: Select your language

The first step in personalising your console is to select your language. If the language you speak is not available, select one that you can read so that you can connect your console to the internet and begin your first system update. After the update is complete, more languages may be available.

After you select your language, press the A button to continue with your digital setup.

Step 3: Select your language variety

After you pick your language, you’ll be asked to identify which region of that language you use. Selecting the correct region of the language you chose will help voice navigation to understand you better and to adjust the spelling and grammatical selection of text shown on screen to match your expectations.

After you select your language region, press the A button  to continue with your digital setup.

Step 4: Change your display settings (if prompted to do so)

In rare cases, the console may not be able to automatically determine your TV’s resolution. If this is the case, you’ll see the Change your display screen. Manually select your screen’s resolution and press the A button to accept the change.

If you need additional help with this process, see: The display trouble-shooter appears Xbox console setup

If the Change your display screen does not appear, skip this step and continue with your digital setup.

Step 5: Connect your console to the internet

The next step is to connect your Xbox to the internet. This can be done either through a wired connection or a wireless connection.

If you attached a network cable to your Xbox during physical setup, you’ll go directly to the You’re connected screen. Press the A button to continue with your digital setup.

If the console does not detect a wired connection, you’ll be prompted to select a network. On the Which one’s yours? screen, you can:

1. Use wired connection. If you have a network cable attached and select this option, the Network Connection Trouble-shooter will launch so you can determine why the console did not connect automatically.

2. Select from the list of wireless networks. The names of the wireless networks that the console can detect will be listed in order from strongest to weakest signal strength. If you have a wireless network in your home, in most cases it should appear near the top of the list.

3. Add wireless network. Select this option if your wireless network does not appear on the list of available networks. If you select this option, you’ll need to manually enter the following information about your wireless network:

-SSID (network name)

-Security type

-Password

For more info or help with connecting your console to the internet, see: Troubleshoot network connection errors

After you select your network, you’ll see the It’s all good screen. Press the A button to continue with your digital setup.

Step 6: Select your location

Select the country where you live and press the A button to continue with your digital setup. This selection doesn’t affect the language you already chose. Instead, it sets the location for the console and the Microsoft Store. Setting the correct location ensures that you only see content that’s available and playable in your region.

After you select your location, press the A button to continue with your digital setup.

Step 7: Update your system

Once you’re connected to the internet, you’ll need to get the most recent system update so you can use your Xbox for the first time. Review the size of the update and select Start update to begin the download.

If you choose to wait until later (you might have internet usage limits or bandwidth charges that you need to consider), select Turn off console. If you choose this option, your setup experience will pick up where it left off when you turn the console back on.

If you receive an error during the update process, see: Troubleshoot system updates on Xbox

Once the system update is complete, the console will restart.

Note that system updates can take anywhere from 15 minutes to more than an hour to complete. If you want to, you can continue configuring your digital setup while the console is still processing the system update. To do this, use the Xbox app on your iOS or Android mobile device. For more info, see: Complete console setup using the Xbox mobile app

Otherwise, wait for the system update to complete before you continue with the next step.

Step 8: Sign in

Note that if the Xbox service is unavailable, you won’t be able to sign in until the service is restored. In the meantime, you can select Next to go to the Xbox Home screen without being signed in. You’ll still be able to set up your live TV, watch a Blu-ray Disc, or install a game.

Your console can also function while offline. To find out which features are available when it’s offline, see: Using your Xbox console offline
